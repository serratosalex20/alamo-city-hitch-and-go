3. Rental Term

  1. Pickup. The rental term begins at the scheduled pickup time of {{pickup_datetime}} at Owner's premises. Equipment will not be released until Renter has (i) signed this Agreement, (ii) provided a valid driver's license, (iii) provided current proof of insurance coverage that extends to the towed Equipment, and (iv) authorized the required payment and security deposit.
  2. Return. Renter must return the Equipment to Owner's premises no later than {{return_datetime}}, in the same condition received, with the same fuel level (if applicable), and with all accessories listed in Section 2.
  3. Rental Block. The rental is billed in the block selected at booking: {{rental_block}} (4, 12, 24, or 36 hours). Time beyond the selected block is governed by Sections 7 (Extensions) and 8 (Late Return).

4. Rental Fees & Payment

  1. Base Rental Fee. Renter shall pay the rental fee of {{rental_fee_amount}} for the selected rental block, plus applicable Texas sales tax ({{tax_amount}}), for a total of {{total_charge}}. The rental fee is charged at the time of booking via the payment method on file.
  2. Payment Method. Renter authorizes Owner to charge the credit or debit card provided at booking for all amounts due under this Agreement, including overtime fees, damage, cleaning, fuel replacement, and any other lawful charges arising from this rental.
  3. Card Match Requirement. The payment card used must be issued in the legal name of Renter as shown on the driver's license provided. Owner reserves the right to refuse a rental for which the card name does not match the driver's license.

Owner will release the hold within three (3) business days after the Equipment is returned and inspected in acceptable condition. If deductions are required (damage, cleaning, fuel, overtime), Owner will capture only the amount owed and release the remainder.

6. Insurance Requirements

  1. Renter's Policy Required. Renter shall maintain, at Renter's sole cost, automobile liability insurance covering the tow vehicle with limits no less than the State of Texas minimum financial responsibility limits, and the policy shall extend coverage to the Equipment being towed.
  2. Proof of Insurance. Prior to release of the Equipment, Renter shall provide legible photographic proof of a current, active insurance policy meeting the requirements of Section 6.1. Proof shall show (i) named insured, (ii) policy number, (iii) effective dates, and (iv) liability limits. Owner will retain a copy of this proof with the rental file.
  3. Renter Is Primarily Liable. Renter's insurance is primary as to any loss, damage, or third-party claim arising out of the use of the Equipment during the rental term. Owner's insurance (if any) is excess and non-contributing.
  4. No Waiver. Owner's acceptance of proof of insurance does not constitute a waiver of any obligation of Renter under this Agreement, nor a representation by Owner that the coverage is adequate.

7. Use of Equipment & Extensions

  1. Permitted Use. The Equipment may be used only for lawful, personal, or commercial hauling consistent with the trailer class rented and its stated load rating. Renter shall operate the Equipment in compliance with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, including the Texas Transportation Code.
  2. Prohibited Uses. Renter shall not, and shall not permit any other person to:
    1. operate the Equipment while under the influence of alcohol or any controlled substance;
    2. exceed the rated load capacity printed on the trailer or stated in Exhibit A;
    3. use the Equipment to transport hazardous materials, illegal cargo, or live animals unless expressly authorized in writing;
    4. take the Equipment outside the State of Texas without Owner's prior written consent;
    5. sublease, assign, or permit use by any person not identified in this Agreement;
    6. modify, repair, paint, or alter the Equipment in any manner; or
    7. use the Equipment for any purpose that would invalidate any insurance coverage applicable to this rental.
  3. Extensions. If Renter wishes to extend the rental term, Renter must request an extension through the Company website, customer dashboard, or by calling Owner before the scheduled return time. Extensions are sold in four (4) hour blocks at {{extension_block_fee}} per block, subject to availability. An approved extension is confirmed by a new return time issued via email or SMS and by a successful charge to the payment card on file.

8. Late Return

  1. Late Fee. If the Equipment is not returned by the scheduled return time, and Renter has not obtained an approved extension under Section 7.3, Renter shall pay a flat late fee of {{late_fee_amount}} per rental, in addition to any additional block charges for the continued use of the Equipment.
  2. Cost of Extending vs. Late. The approved extension rate (Section 7.3) is materially lower than the flat late fee (Section 8.1). Renter is encouraged to request an extension in advance rather than return late.
  3. Abandonment. Equipment not returned within {{abandonment_threshold_hours}} hours of the scheduled return time, and for which Renter cannot be reached, may be deemed abandoned or stolen, at Owner's discretion. Owner may notify law enforcement, file a theft report, and charge Renter the full replacement cost of the Equipment and any accessories.

9. Damage, Loss, & Inspection

  1. Pre-Rental Inspection. Owner and Renter shall jointly inspect the Equipment at pickup and document its condition on Exhibit B (Condition Report). Renter's signature on Exhibit B constitutes agreement that the Equipment was received in the condition shown.
  2. Return Inspection. Upon return, Owner shall inspect the Equipment within two (2) business days. Any damage, loss, missing accessory, excessive soiling, or mechanical issue not present at pickup and not reasonably attributable to normal wear will be documented and charged to Renter at commercially reasonable rates, with photographic evidence provided to Renter via email.
  3. Renter's Responsibility. Renter is responsible for the Equipment from the moment it leaves Owner's premises until it is returned and inspected under Section 9.2. This responsibility includes theft, vandalism, collision, and weather-related damage.

10. Renter's Representations & Warranties

Renter represents and warrants that:

  1. Renter is at least 21 years of age and holds a valid driver's license in good standing;
  2. Renter's tow vehicle is rated, equipped, and maintained to safely tow the trailer class being rented;
  3. The information provided to Owner (name, address, license, insurance) is current and accurate;
  4. Renter is not renting on behalf of a third party not disclosed to Owner;
  5. Renter has read, understood, and agrees to this Agreement in its entirety.

11. Disclaimer of Warranties

The Equipment is provided “as is.” Except as expressly set forth in this Agreement, Owner makes no warranties of any kind, express or implied, including but not limited to the implied warranties of merchantability, fitness for a particular purpose, and non-infringement. Owner's obligation is limited to providing Equipment that has passed its documented pre-rental inspection.

12. Limitation of Liability & Indemnification

  1. Limitation. To the maximum extent permitted by Texas law, Owner's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the total amount paid by Renter under the applicable rental. In no event shall Owner be liable for consequential, incidental, special, exemplary, or punitive damages, including lost profits or lost business opportunities, even if Owner has been advised of the possibility of such damages.
  2. Indemnification. Renter shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Owner, its members, officers, employees, and agents from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to (i) Renter's use of the Equipment, (ii) Renter's breach of this Agreement, (iii) any act or omission of any person operating the tow vehicle or the Equipment during the rental term, or (iv) any third-party claim arising from the foregoing, except to the extent caused by Owner's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

13. Governing Law; Venue; DTPA Acknowledgment

  1. Governing Law. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Texas, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.
  2. Venue. The exclusive venue for any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be the state or federal courts located in Bexar County, Texas. The parties consent to personal jurisdiction in such courts.
  3. DTPA Notice. Renter acknowledges that Renter has had the opportunity to consult with an attorney of Renter's own choosing concerning this Agreement and the rights afforded under the Texas Deceptive Trade Practices – Consumer Protection Act, Texas Business & Commerce Code Chapter 17 (“DTPA”). No modification is effective unless in writing and signed by both parties. If any provision of this Agreement is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions will continue in full force and effect.
