## Patterns of the Top 10%

Five patterns consistently separate the top-scoring sites from the bottom:

### Pattern 1: Price Transparency
Every top competitor shows prices on the page. No "call for pricing." Customers comparison-shop before calling — showing price upfront earns the click and the call.

### Pattern 2: Online Booking or Clear Reservation CTA
Big Boy, Alamo City TR, and Neighbors all have a "Book Now" flow. U-Haul has a reservation engine. Hookup (the outlier) loses points here — their CTA goes to a contact form, not a booking flow. The sites that convert have a clear, low-friction next step.

### Pattern 3: Phone Number + Online Booking Paired
Every top-5 site shows BOTH: a phone number prominently AND a digital booking option. Local customers want the phone option; mobile customers want the online option. Give them both.

### Pattern 4: Social Proof on the Homepage
Big Boy features 3 named Google reviews with star ratings on the homepage. Hookup has 2 named testimonials. U-Haul has brand ubiquity. The weakest sites (SA Beast) have zero social proof. Reviews convert fence-sitters.

### Pattern 5: Real Trailer Photos with Full Specs
The top sites show clear, professional (or at least real) photos of each trailer with dimensions, GVWR, payload capacity, and hitch requirements. Customers need to know if their vehicle can tow the trailer. Sites that answer this question win the booking.

## Gaps & Opportunities for Alamo City Hitch & Go

These are things NO local competitor is doing well that represent open territory:

1. **Premium visual brand** — Every local competitor looks like a DIY WordPress site. A genuinely premium, designed website would stand out dramatically. U-Haul is clean but corporate. No local player has Apple/Stripe-level design.

2. **Clear brand positioning** — "Hitch & Go" is an excellent brand hook — speed, simplicity, ease. No local competitor owns a positioning around *how fast and easy* the rental process is (Big Boy mentions 15 minutes but doesn't build a brand around it).

3. **SEO content** — No local player has content targeting "trailer rental San Antonio TX", "utility trailer rental near me", "how to rent a trailer in San Antonio" etc. This is an open ranking opportunity.

4. **Spanish-language appeal** — SA Beast mentions "Se Habla Español" but doesn't act on it. San Antonio is ~65% Hispanic. A site with genuine bilingual UX would dominate in this demographic.

5. **Trust-first UX** — Nobody shows their actual location, hours, or what the pickup experience looks like. A "Here's exactly what to expect" section would reduce friction dramatically.
